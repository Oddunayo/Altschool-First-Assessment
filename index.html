Authentic Nigerian Jollof

A smoky, spicy, and savory rice dish. This is a staple of west African cuisine. it is tomato-rich rice cooked with fragrant spices and veggies

Recipe Details

  • Prep Time:20 minutes
  • Cook Time:40-50 minutes
  • Servings:6-8 people
  • Difficulty:Medium

Ingredients

  • 3 cups long-grain parboiled rice, rinsed
  • 1 can (400g) tomato chopped or 4 fresh tomatoes, blended
  • 1 red bell pepper blended
  • 1 large onion (half blended with tomatoes, half sliced)
  • 3 tbsp tomato paste
  • 1/2 cup vegetable oil (or a mix of oil and butter)
  • 2–3 cups chicken or vegetable stock (adjust as needed)
  • 2 tsp thyme, 2 bay leaves, 1 tsp curry powder
  • 1–2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, peas) and cooked protein (chicken/shrimp/fish) optional
  • Salt and freshly ground black pepper to taste
  • 2 Knorr or Maggi cubes (seasoning cubes)

Instructions

  1. Prepare the base: Heat oil in a large pot over medium heat. Fry sliced onions until soft and translucent.
  2. Cook tomato mixture: Add tomato paste and fry for 3–5 minutes, then add the blended tomato + pepper mixture. Simmer for 10–15 minutes until it reduces and the oil separates from the tomato paste.
  3. Season: Stir in thyme, curry powder, bay leaves, salt and pepper. Taste and adjust seasoning — the sauce should be well seasoned because rice absorbs flavour.
  4. Add rice & stock: Pour in the rinsed rice and stir to coat every grain with the sauce. Add hot stock just to cover the rice (about 2–3 cups) — do not overfill. Bring to a gentle boil.
  5. Simmer & steam: Reduce heat to low, cover the pot tightly and cook for 20–30 minutes until rice is tender and liquid is absorbed. For a smoky flavour, briefly blast the bottom with high heat for 1–2 minutes (watch carefully) or use the traditional "smoke-in" method with foil and a charcoal piece.
  6. Finish: Gently fluff the rice with a fork, fold in cooked mixed vegetables and protein, then allow to rest 5 minutes off heat before serving.
  7. Serve hot with fried plantains, salad, or your favourite stew.

Tips:

  • Rice type: Parboiled long-grain rice gives the best texture; adjust liquid if using fresh long-grain rice.
  • Smoky flavour: To get the authentic smoky taste, briefly char a piece of charcoal, put it in a small bowl, place inside the pot on top of the rice, drizzle a teaspoon of oil on the coal and cover immediately — remove the coal after 3–4 minutes.
  • Use a tight-fitting lid to prevent steam loss. If your pot leaks steam, reduce heat and check more often.
  • Leftovers reheat well; add a splash of water and cover while reheating to revive moisture.
